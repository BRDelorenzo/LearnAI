SIMPLE PAST OF TO BE – INTERROGATIVE Na aula passada começamos a estudar sobre o verbo TO BE no SIMPLE PAST. LET’S REVIEW! Vamos revisar: SEE SOME EXAMPLES. Veja alguns exemplos: I WAS busy yesterday. (Eu estava ocupado ontem.) You WERE tired last night. (Você estava cansado ontem à noite.) June WASN’T at school last week. (June não estava na escola semana passada.) Brandon and Vinicius WEREN’T sad. (Brandon e Vinícius não estavam tristes.) Agora veremos a forma interrogativa, que, como no SIMPLE PRESENT, é apenas necessário trocar o VERB TO BE de lugar com o PRONOUN. LOOK! Olhe: CHECK OUT SOME EXAMPLES. Verifique alguns exemplos: WAS Mirian furious? (Mirian estava furiosa?) Yes, she WAS. / No, she WASN’T. WERE you and your friend at work last night? (Você e seu amigo estavam no trabalho na noite passada?) Yes, we WERE. / No, we WEREN’T. WAS Mr. Pennington in Paris last year? (O senhor Pennington estava em Paris no ano passado?) Yes, he WAS. / No, he WASN’T. WERE my parents at the market two hours ago? (Meus pais estavam no mercado 2 horas atrás?) Yes, they WERE. / No, they WEREN’T. Lembrando que ainda podemos usar o VERB TO BE com as WH-
words: WHERE WERE you last weekend? (Onde você estava no fim de semana passado?) (Eu estava no shopping com meus amigos.) WHAT WAS his favorite color? (Qual era a cor favorita dele?) His favorite color WAS blue. (A cor favorita dele era azul.) HOW WAS the weather yesterday? (Como estava o tempo ontem?) It WAS sunny. (Estava ensolarado.) WHO WERE you with yesterday? (Com quem voce estava ontem?) Bonney and Tyler WERE with me. (Bonney e Tyler estavam comigo.) I WAS at the mall with my friends.
